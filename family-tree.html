Allison Line — Scottish & Acadian French

William Allison Sr. (~1785, Inverkip, Renfrewshire, Scotland) married Jean Stewart in Greenock in 1811. Their son William Jr. (b. 1812, Inverkip) became a sailor who settled in Cape Breton with Acadian French Louise Fougeau. Their grandson John James Allison (b. 1885, Sydney Mines) settled in Glace Bay, where his daughter Rita Artis Allison (~1918) married Howard Frederick Burchell on 9 May 1949. Full Allison Heritage →

☘️

Connolly Line

John Connolly (~1831, French Village) — Coaster (ran coastal trading ships). Married Leah ___. Their daughter Eliza Jane Connolly (~1860) married John Burchell Jr. on 1 January 1881. Witness at that wedding: Lucy Connolly, likely Eliza's sister. The Connolly and Burchell families were neighbours in French Village.

⛏️

McDonald Line

Alex McDonald (Coal Trimmer, Louisbourg) married Sarah ___. Their daughter Annie McDonald (~1887, Louisbourg) married Ernest Burchell on 12 November 1907 in Sydney. The McDonalds were already embedded in Cape Breton's coal economy before the Burchells arrived — likely part of what drew Ernest to Glace Bay.

🌊

Dauphinee Line

Jean Dauphinee (1722, Montbéliard, France) arrived on the Speedwell in 1752 alongside Pierre Boutilier. His daughter Catherine Dauphinee married Jean George Boutilier ~1785, uniting the two founding families just one generation after their arrival. The Dauphinee name is still found around St. Margaret's Bay today.

❓

Unknown — Husband #1

Susan Boutilier's first husband (~1840–1851) remains unidentified. He fathered three sons who all appear in the 1851 Census as boys under age 10. His surname — carried by those three boys — represents another whole branch of the family tree, still waiting to be discovered in the NS Archives.
