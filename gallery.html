📄 Marriage Certificate · 1949

"Da & Nanny's Marriage Cert"

This is the marriage certificate of Howard Frederick Burchell and Rita Artis Allison, married on the 9th of May, 1949 in Glace Bay, Cape Breton, Nova Scotia. Lovingly called "Da & Nanny's Marriage Cert" in the family, this document connects the older generations to the living ones. Rita brought with her the Allison heritage: Scottish Lowlands (Renfrewshire, Scotland), Acadian French Cape Breton, and coal-country Nova Scotia. Explore the Allison Heritage →

Howard was the son of Ernest William Burchell — the coal miner who worked 35 years for the Dominion Coal Company — and Annie McDonald. His father had died just four years earlier in 1945. Howard gave 677 Arthur Street, Glace Bay, as his address — the same home his father had lived in for decades.

GroomHoward Frederick Burchell
BrideRita Artis Allison
Date9 May 1949
PlaceGlace Bay, Cape Breton, Nova Scotia
Groom's parentsErnest & Annie (McDonald) Burchell
Family address677 Arthur St., Glace Bay

From this marriage came Marie Elaine Burchell, and her son Thomas Bernard Burchell — the keeper of this family's story.

📄 Death Certificate · 1945

Ernest William Burchell — Death Record

The death certificate of Ernest William Burchell, who died on December 8, 1945 at Glace Bay General Hospital. This document was a landmark discovery in the family research — it confirmed Ernest's full name, birth date, parents, occupation, and cause of death in one primary source.

Ernest's son Daniel Burchell was the informant, which introduced a previously unknown family member — Howard's brother — into the tree. The certificate listed Ernest's parents only as "Burchell" (father) and "Unknown" (mother), a common limitation when the informant didn't know the details. His marriage record later filled that gap.

NameErnest William Burchell
Death8 December 1945, Glace Bay General Hospital
Born12 November 1881, Halifax, NS (age 64 yrs 29 days)
Residence677 Arthur St., Glace Bay
OccupationFireman, Dominion Coal Co., 35 years
Cause of deathPulmonary Tuberculosis
WifeAnnie Burchell
InformantDaniel Burchell (son)
BuriedGreenwood Cemetery, Dec. 11, 1945

📄 Marriage Register · 1907

Ernest Burchell & Annie McDonald — 1907

The marriage register entry for Ernest Burchell (age 26, Seaman, Louisbourg) and Annie McDonald (age 20, Louisbourg), married on 12 November 1907 in Sydney, Cape Breton — a Presbyterian ceremony by licence. This document anchors Ernest firmly to the St. Margaret's Bay Burchell family.

The register records Ernest's birthplace as St. Margaret's Bay and his parents as John & Elizabeth Burchell — confirming the direct line from the Irish immigrant John Burchell Sr. and Susan Boutilier. Annie's father was a Coal Trimmer, placing both families squarely in Cape Breton's industrial coal world.

GroomErnest Burchell, 26, Seaman, Louisbourg
BrideAnnie McDonald, 20, Louisbourg

RitePresbyterian, by Licence

📄 Marriage Register · 1881

John Burchell Jr. & Eliza Jane Connolly — 1881

The marriage register for John Burchell Jr. (age 24, Bachelor, Fisherman, French Village) and Eliza Jane Connolly (age 21, Spinster, French Village), married on 1 January 1881 at St. Margaret's Bay, Halifax County — by Church of England banns.

Both John and Eliza were from French Village, and both had parents recorded in the same register: John's parents were John & Susan Burchell; Eliza's parents were John & Leah Connolly. Witnesses were Alex Westhaver and Lucy Connolly. John Jr. is the father of Ernest William Burchell — Howard's father.

GroomJohn Burchell Jr., 24, Fisherman, French Village
BrideEliza Jane Connolly, 21, French Village
Date1 January 1881, St. Margaret's Bay
RiteChurch of England, by Banns
John's parentsJohn & Susan Burchell

📄 Marriage Bond · 1810

Francis Boutilier & Susannah Butler — 1810

The marriage bond of Francis Boutilier and Susannah Butler, dated 4 June 1810, Halifax County, Nova Scotia — held in the Nova Scotia Archives as RG 1, vol. 170, page 78. This is one of the oldest documents in the entire collection, and one of the most important.

Francis Boutilier (born ~1788) was the son of Jean George Boutilier and grandson of Pierre Boutilier, who arrived in Halifax aboard the Speedwell from Montbéliard, France on 4 July 1752 — a founding passenger among the "Foreign Protestant" settlers of Nova Scotia. This bond proves the Church of England marriage and links the family to the Boutilier line that settled at French Village, St. Margaret's Bay.

GroomFrancis Boutilier (~1788–after 1851)
BrideSusannah Butler
Date4 June 1810, Halifax County, NS
WitnessBarnard Munro
AuthorityHis Excellency Sir George Prevost's administration
ArchiveNS Archives RG 1 v.170 p.78

Francis's daughter Susan Boutilier would later marry Irish immigrant John Burchell (~1855), giving rise to the St. Margaret's Bay Burchell family.

📋 Census Record · 1851

Susan Boutilier — 1851 Census, Head of Household

The 1851 Census of Halifax County records "Mrs. Susan Boutilier" as head of household — a widow raising three young sons, with no husband present. This document is a pivotal discovery: it confirms Susan was widowed from her first (unknown) husband before she married Irish immigrant John Burchell around 1855.

Susan appears at M-3138, Page 8, Line 12, Halifax County. She is likely living near another woman aged 30–40 (possibly a sister or in-law). The three sons under age 10 carry an unknown surname — another family branch still to be traced.

HeadMrs. Susan Boutilier (widow)
Census1851, Halifax County, M-3138, Page 8 Line 12
HouseholdThree sons under age 10; no husband present
SignificanceConfirms prior marriage; widowed before ~1851
Next known eventm2. John Burchell ~1855, French Village

Historical Context & Heritage

Images and context that bring the family's world to life

⛵ The Speedwell, 1752
The ship Speedwell carried your ancestors to Halifax on 4 July 1752 — Pierre Boutilier and Jean Dauphinee among the passengers.
🇫🇷 Montbéliard, France
Montbéliard, eastern France — birthplace of Pierre Boutilier (1720) and Jean Dauphinee (1722). A Protestant island in a Catholic sea.
⚓ St. Margaret's Bay
St. Margaret's Bay, Nova Scotia — home to five generations of Boutiliers and Burchells. Boutilier's Point still bears the family name.
⛏️ Glace Bay Collieries
Glace Bay's Dominion Coal Company collieries — where Ernest Burchell worked for 35 years. The coal mines defined a generation of Cape Breton families.
Kilmoe, Co. Cork, Ireland — John Burchell's ancestral parish. The Famine drove him to Nova Scotia, where he married Susan Boutilier.
Kilmoe, Co. Cork — the rugged landscape John Burchell left behind during the Great Famine, 1845–1852.
📜 Marriage Bond 1810
The 1810 Marriage Bond of Francis Boutilier and Susannah Butler — NS Archives RG 1 v.170 p.78 — one of the oldest documentary traces of the family.
