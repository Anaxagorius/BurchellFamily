📄 Marriage Certificate · 1949

"Da & Nanny's Marriage Cert"

This is the marriage certificate of Howard Frederick Burchell and Rita Artis Allison, married on the 9th of May, 1949 in Glace Bay, Cape Breton, Nova Scotia. Lovingly called "Da & Nanny's Marriage Cert" in the family, this document connects the older generations to the living ones. Rita brought with her the Allison heritage: Scottish Lowlands (Renfrewshire, Scotland), Acadian French Cape Breton, and coal-country Nova Scotia. Explore the Allison Heritage →

Howard was the son of Ernest William Burchell — the coal miner who worked 35 years for the Dominion Coal Company — and Annie McDonald. His father had died just four years earlier in 1945. Howard gave 677 Arthur Street, Glace Bay, as his address — the same home his father had lived in for decades.

GroomHoward Frederick Burchell
BrideRita Artis Allison
Date9 May 1949
PlaceGlace Bay, Cape Breton, Nova Scotia
Groom's parentsErnest & Annie (McDonald) Burchell
Family address677 Arthur St., Glace Bay

From this marriage came Marie Elaine Burchell, and her son Thomas Bernard Burchell — the keeper of this family's story.

📄 Death Certificate · 1945

Ernest William Burchell — Death Record

The death certificate of Ernest William Burchell, who died on December 8, 1945 at Glace Bay General Hospital. This document was a landmark discovery in the family research — it confirmed Ernest's full name, birth date, parents, occupation, and cause of death in one primary source.

Ernest's son Daniel Burchell was the informant, which introduced a previously unknown family member — Howard's brother — into the tree. The certificate listed Ernest's parents only as "Burchell" (father) and "Unknown" (mother), a common limitation when the informant didn't know the details. His marriage record later filled that gap.

NameErnest William Burchell
Death8 December 1945, Glace Bay General Hospital
Born12 November 1881, Halifax, NS (age 64 yrs 29 days)
Residence677 Arthur St., Glace Bay
OccupationFireman, Dominion Coal Co., 35 years
Cause of deathPulmonary Tuberculosis
WifeAnnie Burchell
InformantDaniel Burchell (son)
BuriedGreenwood Cemetery, Dec. 11, 1945

Historical Context & Heritage

Images and context that bring the family's world to life

⛵ The Speedwell, 1752
The ship Speedwell carried your ancestors to Halifax on 4 July 1752 — Pierre Boutilier and Jean Dauphinee among the passengers.
🇫🇷 Montbéliard, France
Montbéliard, eastern France — birthplace of Pierre Boutilier (1720) and Jean Dauphinee (1722). A Protestant island in a Catholic sea.
⚓ St. Margaret's Bay
St. Margaret's Bay, Nova Scotia — home to five generations of Boutiliers and Burchells. Boutilier's Point still bears the family name.
⛏️ Glace Bay Collieries
Glace Bay's Dominion Coal Company collieries — where Ernest Burchell worked for 35 years. The coal mines defined a generation of Cape Breton families.
☘️ Ireland — The Famine Years
Ireland 1845–1852 — the Great Famine drove John Burchell from his homeland to the fishing villages of Nova Scotia, where he would marry Susan Boutilier.
📜 Marriage Bond 1810
The 1810 Marriage Bond of Francis Boutilier and Susannah Butler — NS Archives RG 1 v.170 p.78 — one of the oldest documentary traces of the family.
