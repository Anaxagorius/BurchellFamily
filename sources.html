Research & Sources

The primary sources and documentary evidence behind the family history

How This Research Was Built

Every fact in this family history is supported by at least one primary source — a census record, a marriage bond, a death certificate, or a church register. The research follows standard genealogical methodology: start with what you know (Ernest 1881), work backwards generation by generation, and cite every source.

This page lists all confirmed primary sources, the 1871 and 1881 census household data, and the remaining open research questions — the mysteries still waiting to be solved.

📜 Primary Sources — Confirmed Documents

Marriage Bond — Francis Boutilier & Susannah Butler

4 June 1810, Halifax County, Province of Nova Scotia. Witnessed by Barnard Munro. Signed before His Excellency Sir George Prevost's administration. Proves Francis Boutilier and Susannah Butler married by Church of England rites.

NS Archives RG 1 v.170 p.78 · ✓ Confirmed

1851 Census — Susan Boutilier, Head of Household

"Mrs. Susan Boutilier" listed as head of household, Halifax County. Three sons under age 10, no husband present. Confirms she was widowed before marrying John Burchell. Likely living with another woman aged 30–40 (possibly a sister or in-law).

1851 Census Halifax Co., M-3138, Page 8 Line 12 · ✓ Confirmed

1871 Census — Birchill Family, French Village

Halifax West, Sub-district H, Page 15. Head: John Birchill, 40, Fisherman, born Ireland, Irish. Wife: Susan Birchill, 52, born NS, French. Sons: John (14), Benjamin (11), William (9), James (7), George (5), Henry (1). Confirms Irish immigrant husband with French Huguenot wife and six Nova Scotia-born sons.

Library & Archives Canada, 1871 Census · ✓ Confirmed

1881 Census — Burchell Family, French Village

Family #91, Page 21. Head: John Burchell, 51, Farmer, born Ireland, Irish, C of E. Wife: Susan Burchell, 60, born NS. Children still at home: John (24), Benjamin (21), William (19), James (17), George (15), Henry (11), Eliza (8). Confirms Susan still alive in 1881 and the full sibling group.

Library & Archives Canada, 1881 Census, Image e008118034 · ✓ Confirmed

1891 Census — Burchill Family, French Village

John Burchill (66), Head, Farmer, born Ireland, C of E, Irish. Susan Burchill (70), Wife, born NS, C of E, French. Benjamin Burchill (31), Son. Confirms both parents still alive in 1891; John now age 66, Susan age 70. John dies by 1894.

Library & Archives Canada, 1891 Census · ✓ Confirmed

Marriage Register — John Burchell Jr. & Eliza Jane Connolly

1 January 1881, St. Margaret's Bay, Halifax County. Anglican, by Banns. Groom: John Burchell, 24, Bachelor, Fisherman, French Village. Parents: John & Susan Burchell. Bride: Eliza Connolly, 21, Spinster, French Village. Parents: John & Leah Connolly. Witnesses: Alex Westhaver, Lucy Connolly.

NS Archives Marriage Register · ✓ Confirmed

Marriage Register — Ernest Burchell & Annie McDonald

12 November 1907, Sydney, Cape Breton. By License, Presbyterian. Groom: Ernest Burchell, 26, Seaman, Louisbourg. Birthplace: St. Margaret's Bay. Parents: John & Elizabeth Burchell. Bride: Annie McDonald, 20, Louisbourg. Parents: Alex & Sarah McDonald (father: Coal Trimmer). Witnesses: Annie Johnson, Sarah McDonald.

Marriage register, Sydney, Cape Breton, 1907 · ✓ Confirmed

Death Certificate — Ernest William Burchell

Died 8 December 1945, Glace Bay General Hospital. Age 64 years, 29 days (born 12 November 1881). Residence: 677 Arthur St., Glace Bay. Occupation: Fireman, Dominion Coal Co., 35 years. Cause: Pulmonary Tuberculosis. Wife: Annie Burchell. Informant: Daniel Burchell (son). Buried: Greenwood Cemetery, 11 Dec 1945.

Nova Scotia Death Certificate, 1945 · ✓ Confirmed — Document in this collection

Marriage Certificate — Howard Frederick Burchell & Rita Artis Allison

9 May 1949, Glace Bay, Cape Breton, Nova Scotia. Howard's parents: Ernest & Annie Burchell. Address: 677 Arthur St., Glace Bay. This is the document known in the family as "Da Nanny's Marriage Cert."

Nova Scotia Marriage Certificate, 1949 · ✓ Confirmed — Document in this collection

Speedwell Passenger List — Pierre Boutilier & Jean Dauphinee

4 July 1752, Halifax, Nova Scotia. Pierre Boutilier (age ~32), wife Marguerite Tanner, and 3 children. Jean Dauphinee (age ~30), wife Marie Madeleine Mourand, and 2 children. The founding document of the French Huguenot line in Nova Scotia.

PANS (Public Archives of Nova Scotia) vol. 35, doc. 16 · ✓ Confirmed

🔍 Open Research Questions — Mysteries Still To Solve

  1. Who was Susan Boutilier's first husband?
    She married him ~1840–1845, had 3 sons by 1851, and was widowed by 1851. Search NS Archives Marriages: Susan Boutilier + Halifax County + 1840–1850. Those three boys carry an unknown surname — another whole family branch.
  2. Find Francis Boutilier in the 1851 Census.
    He should be ~63, possibly living near Susan (his daughter). That page of the 1851 census should be visible. Finding him confirms his second wife (Elizabeth Kiddy) and half-siblings.
  3. Find John Burchell Sr.'s Irish roots.
    His 1894 death certificate would list his parents — the key to finding his Irish parish. Search: John Burchell + Death + Halifax County + 1894. His will or probate may also exist.
  4. Pull the Hubley line.
    The 1851 census page that shows Susan also shows what appears to be "Mr. Jas. Ehloid" — likely a Hubley — on the same page. This may connect another Nova Scotian family to the tree.
  5. Locate Susan and John Burchell Sr.'s marriage record (~1855).
    Would give Susan's full Boutilier maiden name confirmation and may list witnesses who identify other family connections.
  6. Find Daniel Burchell after 1945.
    Ernest's son Daniel (who reported Ernest's death) lived at King Edward Street, Glace Bay. A census or directory search would fill in another branch of the family.

Continuing the Research

Archives and resources for further investigation

🗂️

Nova Scotia Archives

The NS Archives holds vital statistics, marriage bonds, death certificates, land records, and more. The Francis Boutilier 1810 marriage bond is held here. Essential for the pre-1900 Boutilier and Burchell research.

Visit NS Archives
🍁

Library & Archives Canada

The Canadian census records (1851, 1871, 1881, 1891, 1901) are freely searchable here. All confirmed census data in this family history comes from this source.

Visit LAC
🌳

Genealogy Research

Ancestry.ca and FamilySearch.org hold digitized Nova Scotia records including census microfilm, church registers, and vital statistics. Both are essential tools for the next phase of research.

Visit FamilySearch
📚

The Genealogical Association of NS

GANS maintains records, burial indexes, and provides research assistance specifically for Nova Scotia families. Their published cemetery indexes are invaluable for confirming burials at St. Paul's Anglican, French Village, and Greenwood Cemetery, Glace Bay.

Visit GANS
